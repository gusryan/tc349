critique of panic.com

Panic.com has a nice feel in both look and presentation. The color palate is fresh and the site has a narrow design, suggesting optimization on portrait-oriented iOS devices. Interestingly, the site incorporates Flash, which is not compatible with Apple gadgets. But that doesn't detract from the overall experience. In all, it's cool enough to pass the first test.

The products have merit and serve their respective purposes, as well. Prompt enables the user to connect to other devices over a secure SSH tunnel. Transmit looks to enable other secure transport mechanisms, which are generally preferred over the open channel. Code offers the ability to manipulate web pages from your iOS device. In two flavors. Off the top, I see four out of five apps as being useful to me personally, so I like this site's products. As an aside, Panic also offers t-shirts which harken back to 1982 with seemingly vintage gear.

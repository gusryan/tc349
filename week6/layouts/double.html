Chartbeat appears to be a statistics company that focuses on integrating parsed and company-specific Internet data with a heavy dose of cool. It then packages that data and sells it back to corporations, providing a unique lens into an organization's Internet presence (and prevalence) as it relates to the corporate name and brand. Chartbeat focuses on the Internet's "view" of your corporate brand by monitoring and measuring its presence and activity on the Internet through a variety of channels, including Twitter links and mentions (@) and Site Shares on Facebook. As this data is captured in real time, it's packaged up and rendered to the customer through a jam-packed portal that is loaded with interesting data related to the customer's corporate brand.

The portal itself is a no-frills layout that focuses almost exclusively on the really good stuff: the data. There's tons of negative space, forcing viewer focus onto the data and not the design. The data is pulled in from a variety of vectors and is primarily laid out in a dashboard scheme that provides a quick snapshot of the most relevant data points. From there, users can drill down into various sub-sections to take a closer look at the details. Sections include those that focus on the content being viewed, shared, etc, around the Internet, and the social sites where mentions are being seen (pinterest, stumble upon, facebook, twitter). Additionally, the portal offers another level of detail regarding traffic sources (e.g., top links, top search terms) and also boasts a Geo link, which contributes to the cool factor by reverse engineering the users IP address to map that to the user's "global location" (or at least to that of the nearest CO/POP of the user's ISP).

In all, chartbeat looks like a great tool for organizations to monitor and understand how and where the corporate brand is moving across the Internet. We've learned in other courses that user feedback is among the most important factors people use when forming an opinion about an organization/facebook page/twitter posture, and Chartbeat looks like a viable means for organizations to measure user opinion and feedback on the corporate brand. This in addition to the more transparent use of seeing exactly when and where messaging about the organzation is flowing across the expanses of the ever-changing Internet.
